A lot of people seem to think that by adding more stuff to their products they’re actually increasing their customer base. But really, I find it to be the opposite. The fewer things your product does, the more people can use it. Because it’s less specific, more generic. And that’s what we’ve found to be true. 

— Brilliant insight by Jason Fried, maker of Basecamp, on the lovely podcast “Hired" by Cameron Moll, maker of Authentic Jobs. The emphasis is mine.

We’ve all thought about how less is more. But typically that’s a matter of more easy to use, less fluff. I love the idea that it can also mean it’s more useful to more people.
