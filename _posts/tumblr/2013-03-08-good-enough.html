Good Enough is something we’re taught to be ashamed of. But Good Enough is the Goldilocks of solutions. It’s the right amount of good. 

Let’s examine this.

Like all creative and effortful endeavors, Design is an emotional undertaking. We work hard, we put in time. We get invested. That’s what investment is: the putting in of resources. But investment can make it really difficult to see where true value lies. To see what we’re working towards, instead of what we have to lose.

In so many ways, Design is finding solutions within limited resources – whether they’re budgets, time, users’ needs, stakeholder priorities (ourselves included), technology, whatever. But there’s nuance here that’s so important. The best problem solving isn’t about working within things, be it budgets or time, it’s about conceiving of limitations as fundamental to the solution. The problem, the solution, the limitations. They’re all the same thing. 

Esoteric? 

If you’re reading this on JonAmar.com (and not a feed reader of some sort) take a look around you. The design of this site is … adequate. It’s not particularly pretty. It’s not particularly original. It’s not even fantastically functional. It’s just good enough.

And that’s why I chose it. The problem I was solving when starting this blog didn’t require a fancier solution. I wanted somewhere to share my ideas. That was it. So I didn’t spend a lot of time worrying about having the perfect theme.
